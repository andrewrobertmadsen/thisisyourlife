This is your life

When I was a kid, my parents didn’t talk a lot about money. My Mom and Dad both worked normal jobs and I never went without. My Mom encouraged me to pursue a career in medicine as a doctor but I didn’t really give it much thought until college. I knew I liked biology from high school so I decalred a biology major thinking it a good fit for pre-med and began my course work.

Pre-med was tough, and not just the course work. It required voluteering for many hours in medical settings with little to no monetary compensation. That was something I just couldn’t do coming from a middle class family and being newly married. I had rent to pay and food to provide. So I threw in the towel to persue a degree in chemistry thinking to be a professor.

Around this time my mother's health took a turn for the worse and I realized that right now was not the time that I wanted to head out of state to graduate school. With little prospect of achieving professorship in chemistry by persuing grad school at any of the in-state colleges, I found myself searching for a career to pursue yet again.

I happend upon the field of environmental engineering (a sub-domain of civil engineering), heard it payed an salary of $80,000, and involved a fair bit of chemistry. Having no clue what life cost and that being one of the higher salaries I had heard I decided to pursue a master's degree in the subject.

Environmental engineering was a good decision looking back. It was rigorous and intruduced me to the skill that got me to my current career. I still remember my first job offer in the field. I was offered $23 per hour. I couldn't believe it. I was raking in the dough.

Fast forward 5 years and I was indeed making the $80,000 per year that I had dreamed of with litle prospect of my salary growing much futher than $90,000 in the next 5-10 years. Now don't get me wrong, finacially we were doing okay. We had a house and cars that worked most of the time. But went weren't saving for retirement like we needed to and student loan payments that I knew would crush us were looming on the horizon. With little prospect of a drastically different salary, I began to worry how it was all going to work out.

Cutting to the chase, I made a career pivot to data engineering. It was what I actually did for the engineering firms I worked for already so that made the transition easier. With the career change came a higher salary that allowed us to more comfortably meet our financial demands and even check off a few finacial dreams.

I find myself often reflecting on my career choices that got me hear, wondering if there was a shorter path. I also am constantly amazed at my lack of knowldge about what life actually costs and how much money some people actually make. I know they say that money can't buy you happiness and I knew my fair share of people who are well off and miserable. But I also can't deny that my neighbors look pretty happy tearing around on their side-by-sides and heading out on the new boat to wakeskating.

My purpose in creating this site is a hope that maybe someone younger than I will use it to gain some insight into what life costs, what careers pay enough to deliver the lifestyle they want, and avoid the trap that I fell into of not reconciling income with lifestyle. May you be able to prepare yourself to live out your finacial dreams better than I was prepared is my wish to you all.
